## 1. Definitions
- **"Software"** — the Drawback Engine duty-drawback eligibility and recovery-estimation decision-support

- **"Estimate"** — any computed potential-recovery figure, match, trace, or defensibility report produced
  by the Software.
- **"Licensed Filer"** — a U.S. licensed customs broker or attorney, or an importer/exporter filing a
  drawback claim solely on its own account.

Licensee may use the Software **only** when operated by, or under the direction and judgment of, a
**Licensed Filer**. Specifically, the Software may be used to prepare estimates and claim materials only
(a) by a licensed U.S. customs broker or attorney, or (b) by an importer/exporter for **its own** drawback
claims filed **solely on its own account**. **Licensee shall not use the Software to prepare drawback
determinations or claims on behalf of third parties unless Licensee is a licensed customs broker/attorney
authorized to transact such customs business.** (19 U.S.C. § 1641; 19 CFR Part 111.)

## 4. **[COMPLIANCE] Nature of the Software; no customs business; no advice**
The Software is **decision-support and preparation software only**. It is **not** a customs broker, law
firm, or accountant; it does **not** transact customs business; it does **not** file with U.S. Customs and
Border Protection; and it is **not** a substitute for the advice or services of a Licensed Filer. Every
Estimate is an **estimate, not a determination of eligibility and not a guarantee of any refund**, and is
subject to the independent review and filing of a Licensed Filer. No attorney-client or
customs-broker-client relationship is created by use of the Software.

filing date); and (d) compliance with all applicable customs, export, data-confidentiality, and other laws
