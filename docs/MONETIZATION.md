## 1. The one fact that determines everything

**You can't run a drawback business.** Filing a drawback claim is "customs business" that legally requires a
licensed customs broker/attorney + ABI/ACE (19 CFR 111.1; 19 U.S.C. § 1641). Your own product already
encodes this. With no license, the legally-clean ways for **you** to make money are narrow:

| Model | Legal for an unlicensed party? | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Sell the software/IP outright (asset sale)** | ✅ Clean — code/IP isn't customs business | 19 CFR 111.1 |
| **Flat-fee license / SaaS to importers who self-file** | ✅ Clean — CBP explicitly blesses claimant self-file via certified ABI/CATAIR | CBP Drawback FAQ; 19 U.S.C. 1641(b)(1) "solely on behalf of that person" |
| **Flat-fee license / SaaS to a licensed broker (their production tool)** | ✅ Clean — flat fee, not tied to claims | CBP rulings H276784, H290002 (flat = OK) |
| **Contingency % of recovered duty, charged for doing the work** | ❌ Unlicensed customs business | 19 CFR 111.1 (drawback named); HQ H326926 |
| **Transaction-tied rev-share / per-claim cut from a broker** | ❌ Prohibited fee-splitting — taints the *broker* | **19 CFR 111.36(b)**; H276784, H290002 |

**Implication:** every dollar must be **flat** (sale price, flat license, per-seat, per-filing) — **never a
percentage of refunds or a per-claim cut.** That single rule keeps you legal and keeps brokers' compliance
teams from refusing the deal. (Get a customs attorney to paper any fee structure before signing — cheap
insurance.) Tighten the UI copy to "automated estimate — not a determination of eligibility; a licensed
filer must validate," to stay clear of customs-business / unauthorized-practice-of-law risk.

## 2. The strategy: a dual-track, ~12–16-week process

Two tracks in parallel — one for fast cash + a reference, one for the real upside. Plus a slow third option.

### Track A (fast cash + proof) — a PAID pilot with ONE boutique licensed broker
- **Target first:** **Alliance Drawback Services** (boutique, licensed, automation-leaning, small enough that
  your engine is leverage not a threat). Then, if needed, J.M. Rodgers (100%-audit culture → values the audit
  trace) or Charter (down-market SMB funnel they don't serve).
- **Structure:** a **paid, flat-fee, fixed-window pilot** (a few thousand dollars, 60–90 days, defined success
  criteria — e.g., "engine reproduces the broker's manual recovery within X% on N historical files and emits a
  CATAIR file the filer accepts"). **Charge for it** — free pilots signal desperation and rarely convert.
- **Why:** it's your **fastest realistic first dollar** and turns the demo into a **broker-endorsed reference**
  that dissolves the "no customers" gap for Track B. Position as *de-risking their existing service*, not
  competing with their license.

### Track B (the upside) — a PARALLEL "quiet mini-auction" of the funded AI-native entrants
- **Targets, contacted in the SAME week** to manufacture competitive tension: **Pax AI** (strongest — $4.5M
  seed, Flexport-tied, roadmap *is* this asset), **Zollback** (has in-house brokers + paying customers + a
  Descartes-veteran CTO who'll evaluate the engine fast — ideal license/acqui-hire), **Tariff Refund HQ**
  (attorney-gated — fills your legal gap), **Dutywise** (modern drawback brokerage).
- **Outcome:** an **asset sale** or **acqui-hire** (if you'd join) or a **flat-fee engine license**.
- **The honest line that creates leverage:** *"I'm talking with a few teams in the space about licensing or
  acquiring the engine; I'll move with whoever sees the fit fastest."* True, and it converts a no-leverage solo
  seller into a process. **Never negotiate against a single buyer.**

### Track C (slow upside, start now don't wait) — strategic software acquirers
- **Descartes Systems Group** (serial trade-software acquirer, ~51 deals; a self-serve drawback module is a
  genuine portfolio gap). Reach the **product/GM who owns the customs line**, not the M&A mailbox; lead with
  "integrate-in-months capability vs a multi-year build." Also watch **Thomson Reuters ONESOURCE** and
  **WiseTech/e2open**. **Caveat:** these run 6–12-month processes and Descartes sits *above* its own stated
  acquisition floor (it buys revenue), so treat as license/partner upside, not your lifeline.

## 3. Honest valuation expectations (don't inflate)

No revenue → **ARR multiples don't apply.** Value rests on strategic/IP basis (engineering-months saved,
domain moat, acqui-hire $/engineer).

| Outcome | Realistic range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Marketplace listing (Acquire.com/Flippa) | **~$5K–$50K (mostly sub-$25K)** | Financial buyers underwrite cash flow you lack. **Use only as a published floor/BATNA — not the plan.** |
| Flat-fee broker license | **low-5 to low-6 figures / yr** once one broker validates in production | Compounds across non-exclusive licensees, but each adds solo-support burden. Flat fees only. |
| Asset sale to a motivated strategic (competitive process) | **~$150K–$750K** | Requires the parallel mini-auction; premiums exist only when a buyer fears a rival getting it. |
| Acqui-hire you personally join | **$1M+ total comp** | Mostly salary + vesting + retention over years, **not** upfront cash. Only if you'll take the job. |

The spread between the floor and the ceiling is **entirely** a function of reaching the 4–6 right buyers
directly and packaging the asset well — not of the code's intrinsic worth.

**Package to maximize price (the product is the diligence):**
1. **"Clean-room IP, built from public law."** Solo-built, no contractors, no attorney work-product or broker
   license entangled → **fully assignable, zero title risk.** This neutralizes the #1 M&A deal-killer — lead
   with it.
2. **"Validated correctness engine + test suite + per-line audit trace."** Sell a *proven computation engine
   with citations in a domain where being wrong is a legal liability*, not "an app."
3. **"The glass-box moat incumbents lack + CATAIR done."** Quantify it: *≈12–18 engineering-months of
   validated, regulation-specific work, available now.*

## 4. The $0-budget engine: the demo IS the proof

The product replaces the traction you don't have — for these buyers, a working glass-box demo is stronger
proof than three logo customers.
- **Ungate the live demo** (public link) and make it the center of every conversation; offer to *run their
  sample data live on the call.*
- **One SEO page** targeting "duty drawback eligibility calculator" — a single ranking page can own a niche
  term and pull inbound buyers/partners at $0.
- **A build-in-public LinkedIn teardown** ("why most importers never claim the drawback they're owed") with a
  screen recording of the per-line audit trace, posted into trade-compliance communities (ICPA-type circles).
  Inbound interest is the cheapest way to seed the bidding dynamic and reset the frame from "desperate seller"
  to "interesting inbound."
- **Cold outreach:** founder-to-founder email + LinkedIn mirror, <120 words, lead with the demo link, "advice
  not a sale" open for partners / "license vs keep going alone" for acquirers; 4 touches per target (most
  replies come on touch 2–3).

## 5. Sequence

1. **Weeks 0–2 — Stage the asset.** Ungate demo; record a 60-sec audit-trace screencast; ship one SEO page +
   one LinkedIn teardown; write the three outreach templates; (optional) list on Acquire.com purely to open an
   inbound channel + establish a floor.
2. **Weeks 2–8 — Both tracks live.** Track A: pursue the Alliance paid pilot (fastest first dollar). Track B:
   open the four-way AI-entrant sequences the same week.
3. **Weeks 4–12 — Track C.** Start Descartes / TR / WiseTech product-owner outreach (6–12-mo cycle).
4. **Weeks 8–16 — Convert & leverage.** The pilot result becomes the proof point you forward to every acquirer
   and the trigger to create competition.

**Most-likely timing:** first dollar from the **broker pilot (wk 4–8)**; the larger license/sale from the
**AI-entrant process (wk 8–16+)**; Descartes, if it lands, is 6–12-month upside.

## 6. What NOT to do
- **Don't operate a drawback service** (illegal without a license).
- **Don't take a % of refunds or a per-claim rev-share** (unlicensed customs business / prohibited
  fee-splitting — and brokers will refuse it).
- **Don't raise VC** (you don't want to, and the field is already funded — you'd be late and undercapitalized).
- **Don't rely on a marketplace listing for real money** (caps near $7.5K–$25K) — use it only as a floor.
- **Don't wait.** Flexport (Feb 2026), Pax, and Zollback are already shipping instant/glass-box-adjacent
  drawback. The wedge is real but no longer unique; the asset is worth **most now**, as a build-vs-buy
  time-saver, and depreciates as they finish building.
